## ❌ The Don'ts: What NOT to do

Many people act on instinct after a flood, but these common mistakes can destroy a car instantly.

1. **Don't try to start the engine.** If water has entered the air intake or the cylinders, turning the key can cause hydrolock. Because water does not compress, the pressure will snap piston rods and crack the engine block. If you suspect water reached the engine level, keep the ignition off.
2. **Don't ignore the "chocolate milkshake."** Check your oil dipstick. If the oil looks milky or like a chocolate milkshake, water has mixed with your engine oil. Driving with contaminated oil will cause permanent friction damage to your engine's internal parts.
3. **Don't turn on the AC.** Floodwater is often contaminated. If water entered your AC vents, turning the system on will blow mould spores and bacteria directly into your cabin, creating a health hazard and a permanent bad smell.

## ✅ The Do's: Immediate steps to take

If your car was affected by the UAE rains, follow this checklist immediately:

1. **Disconnect the battery.** Modern cars are rolling computers. Disconnecting the battery prevents short circuits in the ECU (Engine Control Unit) and other sensitive sensors while the car is drying out. If you aren't sure how to do this safely, call a professional.
2. **Check your lights and electricals.** Water often gets trapped inside headlight and taillight housings. This can blow your bulbs or corrode the sockets. Ensure these are drained and dried before use.
3. **Seek a professional flush.** Even if the car starts, "hidden" water in the fuel line or transmission can cause failure weeks later. A mobile oil change and a fluid inspection are the cheapest insurance policies you can buy for a flooded car.
