The open road beckons, offering adventures from the shimmering skylines of Dubai to the serene deserts of Al Ain. Embarking on a road trip across the diverse Emirates is exhilarating, but a smooth journey requires careful preparation. An impromptu breakdown — particularly under the harsh UAE sun — is an experience best avoided. A comprehensive UAE road trip checklist isn't merely a suggestion; it's a necessity.

## Start with vehicle health

Before any long journey, especially departing from the city, inspect your car battery. The intense heat and stop-start city traffic put real strain on it, and a failing battery can quickly turn an adventure into a predicament.

Check for correct voltage and clean terminals. If you're unsure about the health of your unit, schedule a professional test before you hit the open road towards Al Ain or Abu Dhabi. Tyres, engine oil, and fluid levels (coolant, brake fluid) should also be meticulously checked.

## Pack a well-stocked emergency kit

A well-stocked kit is an invaluable companion. Essential items include:

- Plenty of drinking water — especially crucial in the UAE heat
- A first-aid kit
- Jumper cables, a flashlight, and a basic toolkit
- Enough fuel for your route (though our emergency fuel delivery service is available should you miscalculate)
- Fully charged communication devices and a portable power bank

## Plan your route and your stops

Knowing your stops is another critical aspect. Familiarising yourself with rest areas, petrol stations and points of interest along your route ensures a more relaxed and efficient trip. It's also important to observe local traffic regulations and speed limits, as these can vary slightly between Emirates.
