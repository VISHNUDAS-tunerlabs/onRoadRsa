### Flooded Car Recovery in the UAE: Essential Do’s and Don’ts to Save Your Vehicle

The UAE recently experienced unprecedented heavy rainfall, leaving many car owners facing a nightmare: water damage. Whether your car was partially submerged in a parking lot or you had to drive through deep water, the actions you take in the next 24 hours can mean the difference between a simple repair and a total engine loss.

At OnCall Roadside Assistance, we’ve been on the front lines helping drivers across Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Sharjah. To help you protect your investment, we’ve put together this expert guide on the “Silent Killers” of flooded cars repair UAE.

❌ The “Don’ts”: What NOT to do
Many people act on instinct after a flood, but these common mistakes can destroy a car instantly.

1. DON’T Try to Start the Engine
If water has entered the air intake or the cylinders, turning the key can cause Hydrolock. Because water does not compress, the pressure will snap piston rods and crack the engine block. If you suspect water reached the engine level, keep the ignition off.

2. DON’T Ignore the “Chocolate Milkshake”
Check your oil dipstick. If the oil looks milky or like a chocolate milkshake, water has mixed with your engine oil. Driving with contaminated oil will cause permanent friction damage to your engine’s internal parts.

3. DON’T Turn on the AC
Floodwater is often contaminated. If water entered your AC vents, turning the system on will blow mold spores and bacteria directly into your cabin, creating a health hazard and a permanent bad smell.

✅ The “Do’s”: Immediate Steps to Take
If your car was affected by the UAE rains, follow this checklist immediately:

1. Disconnect the Battery
Modern cars are rolling computers. Disconnecting the battery prevents short circuits in the ECU (Engine Control Unit) and other sensitive sensors while the car is drying out. If you aren’t sure how to do this safely, call a professional.

2. Check Your Lights and Electricals
Water often gets trapped inside headlight and taillight housings. This can blow your car bulbs or corrode the sockets. Ensure these are drained and dried before use.

3. Seek a Professional Flush
Even if the car starts, “hidden” water in the fuel line or transmission can cause failure weeks later. A mobile oil change and a fluid inspection are the cheapest insurance policies you can buy for a flooded car.

### Beat the Heat: Essential Car Care Tips to Prevent Summer Breakdowns in the UAE

The UAE summer brings with it intense heat, a formidable adversary for even the most robust vehicles. While we all cherish the convenience of our cars, overlooking essential maintenance during these scorching months can swiftly lead to an unexpected breakdown. No one wants to be stranded on the roadside when the mercury soars. Taking proactive steps can, however, significantly reduce the risk. Let’s explore vital car care tips to ensure your vehicle remains reliable throughout the season.

The heart of your car’s electrical system, the car battery at home or on the road, faces immense strain in extreme temperatures. It is often the first component to show signs of weakness. High temperatures accelerate the evaporation of battery fluid and can corrode internal components. Therefore, regular checks are not merely advisable; they are crucial. Your battery terminals should be kept clean and free of corrosion, and its charge level ought to be periodically assessed. Furthermore, if your battery is nearing its typical lifespan – generally 3-5 years – it might be wise to consider a replacement before the summer truly sets in.

Beyond the battery, your cooling system plays an equally critical role in combating the heat. The engine’s temperature is meticulously regulated by coolant circulating through the radiator. An insufficient coolant level, or a compromised cooling system, can quickly lead to overheating, a highly damaging scenario for your engine. It is imperative that your coolant reservoir is topped up with the correct type of fluid, and a professional inspection for any leaks in the hoses or radiator should be routinely performed.

Another often-underestimated component facing the brunt of summer heat is your tires. The relentless sun radiates heat onto the asphalt, which is then absorbed by your tires. This can cause the air pressure inside to increase, potentially leading to overinflation or, more dangerously, a blowout if the tire is already worn. Therefore, tire pressure should be checked weekly, ideally when the tires are cold. Additionally, the tread depth and overall condition of each tire, including the spare, must be inspected for any signs of wear, cracks, or bulges.

Finally, the air conditioning system, while providing comfort, also requires attention. If your AC isn’t cooling as efficiently as it once did, it could be an indication of low refrigerant levels or other issues. A fully functional AC system not only keeps you comfortable but also helps to reduce the strain on your engine that can result from trying to cool a very hot cabin. Getting your AC system professionally checked and recharged, if necessary, is a small investment for a significant return in comfort and vehicle health.

In conclusion, preventing summer breakdowns in the UAE involves a combination of vigilant checks and timely maintenance. While Oncall Roadside Assistance is always ready to help when unforeseen issues arise, being prepared can save you time, stress, and potential repair costs. Remember, a little proactive care goes a long way in ensuring your journeys remain smooth and uninterrupted, even when the heat is at its most intense.

### Beat the Heat: 5 Critical Battery Tips for UAE Drivers

In the UAE, your car battery faces a brutal environment. Extreme heat accelerates the internal chemical breakdown, evaporation of electrolyte fluid, and corrosion, making premature failure a constant risk. Don’t wait for the engine to fail—use these five targeted tips to protect your battery from the desert sun.

1. Prioritise ‘Shade Hunting’ and Insulation
Direct sun exposure turns your car’s engine bay into an oven, vastly increasing the battery’s internal temperature and speeding up degradation. This is the number one cause of short battery life in the region.

Tip: Always prioritise shaded or covered parking. If forced to park outdoors, invest in a battery thermal blanket or insulator (easily available in the UAE). This simple shield can dramatically lower the operating temperature and extend the battery’s life.
2. Manage Evaporation in Maintenance Batteries
For older or specific non-sealed (flooded lead-acid) batteries, the intense heat causes the battery’s water-based electrolyte to evaporate faster. Once the electrolyte level drops below the plates, the battery capacity is permanently damaged.

Tip: If your battery is not a “maintenance-free” type, regularly check the fluid levels (especially during summer) and only top up with distilled water to the manufacturer’s specified line.
3. Check Voltage Under Maximum AC Load
The heavy demand of running the A/C at full blast in the heat puts maximum stress on both the battery and the alternator (charging system). A failing alternator is a frequent cause of battery death.

Tip: Periodically check your alternator’s performance with the engine running and the A/C set to maximum. A multimeter should show a reading between 13.8 and 14.5 volts. A reading outside this range means the battery isn’t being charged correctly, indicating a potential alternator fault that needs immediate professional attention.
4. Pre-Check Before the Summer Peak
Most battery failures occur during the transition into or peak of summer. The damage happens subtly, and the high temperatures finally trigger the breakdown.

Tip: Treat the change of season as a mandatory service point. Have a mechanic perform an annual load test on the battery before the extreme heat arrives (around May/June). A load test checks the battery’s ability to hold a charge under stress, giving you advance notice of an imminent failure.
5. Clean the Terminal ‘Corrosion Insulator’
While cleaning corrosion is a universal tip, the combination of heat and humidity in coastal areas of the UAE accelerates the build-up of the crusty, white-blue sulfate deposits. This crust acts like an insulator, preventing the battery from receiving a full charge from the alternator.

Tip: Clean terminals thoroughly with a wire brush and a baking soda/water paste. Crucially, after tightening the clamps, apply a thick layer of anti-corrosion terminal grease or spray to create a protective seal against the humid air.
By directly addressing the heat stress and managing the accelerated aging process, you can maximize your battery’s lifespan and avoid getting stranded in the UAE’s challenging climate.
